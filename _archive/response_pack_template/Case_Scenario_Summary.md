# Case Scenario Summary

## Overview
This case involves a mid-size company retirement plan audit failure discovered during Q1 2024. The failure stems from a payroll vendor transition that occurred on February 1, 2024, resulting in late deposits and missed employee deferrals.

---

## Company Profile
- **Company Size:** Mid-size (approximately 200-500 employees)
- **Plan Type:** 401(k) with employer match
- **Payroll Frequency:** Biweekly
- **Plan Year:** Calendar year (2024)

## Compliance Issues Identified

### Issue 1: Late Deposits
- **Regulatory Basis:** ERISA Section 2510.3-102 / DOL Regulation
- **Description:** All deposits made 1-4 days after the required deposit date (7 business days from pay date)
- **Periods Affected:** All 6 periods
- **Total Late Days:** 17 days across 6 periods
- **Exposure:** Lost earnings calculation required

### Issue 2: Missed Employee Deferrals
- **Regulatory Basis:** ERISA Fiduciary Duty / DOL Regulation
- **Description:** David Thompson's contributions not posted to recordkeeper for periods 3-6
- **Periods Affected:** Periods 3, 4, 5, 6
- **Total Missing Contributions:** $2,310.00 (4 periods × $577.50)
  - Employee Deferrals: $1,540.00 (4 × $385.00)
  - Employer Match: $770.00 (4 × $192.50)
- **Root Cause:** Employee ID mapping error during payroll vendor transition
- **Impact:** Participant account underfunded, lost earnings exposure

### Issue 3: Deposit vs. Posting Mismatch
- **Description:** Deposits made for full contribution amounts, but recordkeeper only posted partial amounts
- **Periods Affected:** Periods 3, 4, 5, 6
- **Variance:** $577.50 per period × 4 periods = $2,310.00
- **Impact:** Funds deposited but not allocated to participant accounts

## Key Findings

1. **Vendor Transition Impact:** The payroll vendor change on February 1, 2024, caused a systematic failure in employee ID mapping, resulting in David Thompson's contributions not being transmitted to the recordkeeper.

2. **Late Deposit Pattern:** All deposits were made 1-4 days late, with the pattern worsening after the vendor transition (periods 3-6 were 3-4 days late).

3. **Unallocated Funds:** $2,310.00 was deposited to the plan trust but not allocated to participant accounts, creating a fiduciary compliance issue.

4. **Participant Impact:** David Thompson's account is underfunded by $2,310.00 plus lost earnings, requiring correction.

---

## Required Corrections

1. **Post Missing Contributions:** Allocate $2,310.00 to David Thompson's account for periods 3-6
2. **Calculate Lost Earnings:** Determine lost earnings on:
   - Late deposits (all periods)
   - Missing contributions (periods 3-6)
3. **Deposit Lost Earnings:** Make corrective contributions including lost earnings
4. **Process Improvements:** Implement controls to prevent similar issues in future vendor transitions
